Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive banking details. It usually begins when a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or messaging app, pretending to be a bank official, customer care agent, or even a buyer/seller in an online transaction.

The process typically involves several steps. First, the fraudster gains the victim’s trust by creating a sense of urgency or offering a fake benefit (such as a refund or prize). Next, they send a payment request or a malicious link and instruct the victim to “approve” it to receive money. In reality, approving the request transfers money from the victim’s account. In some cases, victims are also asked to share OTPs or install screen-sharing apps, which further compromises their security.

Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payments are common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and reduced trust in digital systems.

Such frauds can be prevented by never sharing OTPs, avoiding unknown links, and remembering that receiving money does not require approval of a payment request. Awareness and cautious digital behavior are key to staying safe.
